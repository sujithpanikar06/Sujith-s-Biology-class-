Central Nervous System

The Central Nervous System (CNS)

The nervous system consists of two major divisions:

  • Central Nervous System (CNS): Brain and spinal cord
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Sensory and motor neurons

Functions of the CNS

  • Coordinates all body activities
  • Processes and analyzes information
  • Relays messages between body parts
  • Stores information (memory)

The Brain

The brain contains over 100 billion neurons and acts as the control center of the body.

Major Parts of the Brain

  • Cerebrum: Thinking, memory, learning, speech, voluntary movements, sensory perception
  • Cerebellum: Balance, posture, coordination, smooth muscle movements
  • Brain Stem: Connects brain to spinal cord
  • Medulla Oblongata: Controls breathing, heart rate, blood pressure, reflexes
  • Pons: Relays signals and regulates breathing
  • Hypothalamus: Maintains homeostasis (temperature, thirst, hunger, sleep)

The Spinal Cord

  • Extends from brain to lower back
  • Protected by vertebrae
  • Controls reflex actions
  • Connects CNS to the rest of the body
